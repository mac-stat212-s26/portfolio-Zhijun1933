Advanced Spatial Visualization: Minnesota Mapping

ICA 04 — Spatial Data with sf, Elevation Rasters, and Multi-Layer Maps

Author

Zhijun He

Published

February 11, 2026

Data Story Critique

Source: USGS Drought Timeline Visualization

What is the data story? The visualization tracks the spatial and temporal evolution of drought conditions across the United States, showing how drought intensity and coverage have changed over time at the county level.

What is effective? The timeline scrubber allows users to animate drought progression, making temporal patterns intuitive. The color encoding for drought severity (D0–D4) follows a clear sequential palette, and the geographic granularity at the county level reveals regional patterns that state-level data would miss.

What could be improved? The visualization could benefit from an accompanying line chart showing aggregate drought area over time, giving users a macro-level summary alongside the geographic detail. Adding the ability to click on a county for a local time series would also enhance exploratory analysis.

Custom Function with Title Argument

transform_and_plot <- function(spatial_obj, new_crs, title = "Transformed Map") {
  spatial_obj |>
    st_transform(crs = new_crs) |>
    ggplot() +
    geom_sf() +
    theme_classic() +
    labs(title = title)
}

scale_color_viridis_c() notes:

The viridis scales are perceptually uniform, meaning they are readable in color, in greyscale, and by viewers with common forms of color blindness. The _d() variant handles discrete/categorical variables, _c() handles continuous variables, and _b() bins a continuous variable into a small number of discrete color steps.
